When to Go

The best times for comfortable travel and beautiful scenery

Rhine River Cruise: Late September – Mid October

Fall colors turn the Rhine Valley golden and red. Temperatures are mild (60-70°F), summer crowds have thinned, and you avoid peak-season pricing. Early September also works well.

Scottish Highlands: September

The sweet spot. Summer crowds have departed, weather is still pleasant (55-65°F), and the heather blooms purple across the hillsides. Early October works but gets cooler and rainier.

Canadian Rockies: Early – Mid September

Summer crowds thin, fall colors begin, and wildlife is active before winter. The Rocky Mountaineer runs through mid-October. Temperatures are comfortable for exploring.

Booking Timeline

For Fall 2027: Bookings typically open 18 months ahead (Spring 2026). Early booking often gets the best cabin selection and may include promotions.

Getting Ready

What to expect and how to prepare

What You'll Need to Do

  • Walking 1-2 miles at a leisurely pace with rest stops. Some cobblestones and gentle inclines. "Easy pace" excursions always available.
  • Stairs Climb 12-15 steps with handrail (rest at top is fine). River cruise ships have elevators between main decks.
  • Standing 20-30 minutes during guided tours. Benches and rest opportunities usually available.
  • Balance Basic balance on a gently moving ship. River cruises have minimal motion — much less than ocean cruises.

Building Up (3-6 Months Before)

  • Daily Walks Start with 15-20 minutes, build to 30-45. Comfortable pace where you can hold a conversation.
  • Stair Practice If you have stairs at home, practice going up and down once or twice daily. Use the handrail.
  • Standing Practice Practice standing for longer periods — while cooking, at a counter, or during a museum visit.
  • Doctor Check-in Consult your doctors before booking. They can advise on precautions and travel medications.

On the Trip: Take It at Your Pace

Skip what you want. The ship is a comfortable home base — some days, watching castles glide by from the deck is perfect.

Choose "easy" options. Most cruise lines offer tiered excursions. Select "gentle pace" or bus-based tours when available.

Build in rest. A morning excursion followed by an afternoon on the ship is a good rhythm.

Comfortable shoes. Bring well-broken-in walking shoes with good support. Cobblestones are unforgiving.
